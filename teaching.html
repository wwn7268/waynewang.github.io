Teaching Philosophy

I believe that the intersection of mathematics, statistics, and biology offers one of the most powerful frameworks for understanding complex natural phenomena. In my courses — whether teaching probability theory, statistical computing, or bioinformatics — I emphasize building a solid quantitative foundation while cultivating the ability to translate real-world biological problems into rigorous mathematical and computational models.

My teaching integrates theory with hands-on practice. In mathematical modeling and statistical computing courses, students learn not only theorems and algorithms, but also how to implement them using modern computational tools to analyze real datasets. In bioinformatics and AI biology, I guide students to bridge the gap between biological intuition and data-driven discovery, fostering interdisciplinary thinking essential for cutting-edge research.

I strive to create a classroom environment where curiosity is encouraged and mistakes are treated as opportunities for deeper understanding. My goal is to equip students with the analytical skills, programming proficiency, and scientific mindset needed to tackle open-ended problems at the frontier of quantitative biology.
