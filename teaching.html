Teaching Philosophy

I believe that the best way to learn computer science is by building real systems. In my courses, students work on substantial programming projects that mirror the challenges faced by practitioners in industry and research. I emphasize both theoretical foundations and practical implementation skills.

My goal is to help students develop the intellectual tools to reason about complex systems, the technical skills to implement them, and the confidence to tackle open-ended problems. I strive to create an inclusive classroom environment where every student feels encouraged to ask questions and contribute ideas.
