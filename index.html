About Me

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Information Technology at Example University. My research focuses on machine learning systems, distributed computing, and software engineering. I am particularly interested in building efficient, reliable, and scalable systems that bridge the gap between cutting-edge AI algorithms and real-world deployment.

Before joining Example University, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute of Advanced Computing, and I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Tech University in 2018. My work has been recognized with the NSF CAREER Award and best paper awards at leading venues.

I am always looking for motivated students to join my research group. If you are interested in systems and machine learning, please feel free to reach out.

Research Interests

Machine Learning Systems

Designing efficient training and inference frameworks for large-scale models, including distributed training, model compression, and hardware-aware optimization.

Distributed Computing

Building fault-tolerant and high-performance distributed systems for data-intensive applications, with focus on scheduling, resource management, and consistency.

Software Engineering

Investigating program analysis, testing, and verification techniques to improve the reliability and maintainability of complex software systems.
